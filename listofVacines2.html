Vaccine List

  • Oral Polio Vaccine

    Protection from: Poliovirus
    When to give: 6, 10 and 14 weeks

    Oral Polio Vaccine (OPV) protects against poliomyelitis, a disease caused by the poliovirus. Polio is a virus that paralyzes 1 in 200 people who get infected. Among those cases, 5 to 10 per cent die when their breathing muscles are paralyzed. There is no cure for polio once the paralysis sets in.

  • Hepatitis B Vaccine

    Protection From: Hepatitis B
    When to give: At birth

    Hepatitis B virus is a dangerous liver infection that, when caught as an infant, often shows no symptoms for decades. It can develop into cirrhosis and liver cancer later in life. Children less than 6 years old who become infected with the hepatitis B virus are the most likely to develop chronic infections.

  • Pentavalent Vaccine

    Protect From: Diphtheria, Pertussis, Tetanus, Haemophilus Influenzae type b and Hepatitis B
    When to give: 6, 10 and 14 weeks

    Diphtheria infects the nose, throat, tonsils, and skin, potentially causing obstructive pseudo-membranes that hinder breathing and swallowing, and can lead to paralysis, heart failure, kidney failure, or death. Pertussis (whooping cough) results in severe coughing spells lasting weeks, which may cause breathing difficulties, pneumonia, and death. Tetanus causes painful muscle contractions, including lockjaw, making it hard for children to open their mouths or breathe, and can be fatal even with treatment. Haemophilus influenzae type b can lead to meningitis and pneumonia in infants and young children, with transmission occurring via respiratory droplets. 80–90% of infants infected with Hepatitis B in their first year develop chronic infections.

  • Bacillus Calmette-Guérin Vaccine (BCG)

    Protection From: Tuberculosis
    When to give: At birth

    Tuberculosis (TB) is an infection that most often attacks the lungs. In infants and young children, it affects other parts of the body like the brain, bones, joints and other internal organs (extrapulmonary or miliary tuberculosis). A severe case could cause serious complications or death. The BGC vaccine has a protective effect against meningitis and disseminated TB in children. TB is very difficult to treat when contracted, and treatment is lengthy and not always successful. According to the 2020 World Health Organization global TB report, the Philippines has the highest TB incidence rate in Asia, with 554 cases for every 100,000 Filipinos.

  • Pneumococcal Conjugate Vaccine (PCV)

    Protection from: Pneumonia and Meningitis
    When to give: 6, 10 and 14 weeks

    Pneumococcal diseases such as pneumonia and meningitis are a common cause of sickness and death worldwide, especially among young children under 2 years old.

  • Measles, Mumps, Rubella Vaccine (MMR)

    Protection From: Measles, Mumps and Rubella
    When to give: 9 months and 1 year old

    Measles is a highly contagious disease characterized by fever, runny nose, white spots in the mouth, and a rash. Complications can include ear infections, diarrhea, pneumonia, and in severe cases, blindness, brain swelling, or death. Mumps causes headache, malaise, fever, and swollen salivary glands, with complications like meningitis, testicular inflammation, and deafness. Rubella is usually mild in children and adults but can lead to miscarriage, stillbirth, or birth defects in pregnant women (Congenital Rubella Syndrome).
